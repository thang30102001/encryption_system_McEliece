McEliece

General introduction

The McEliece public key cryptosystem is a public key cryptography system developed by Robert J. McEliece in 1978. This system is based on computations in information theory and linear algebra to encode and decode information.

The purpose of using the McEliece system is to secure information by encoding it into an unreadable form until it is decoded with a secret key.

One of the advantages of the McEliece system is its high computational complexity, making it difficult to be attacked by common attack techniques. However, this system also has some limitations, including the very large size of its public and secret keys, and its processing capability is also quite slow.
